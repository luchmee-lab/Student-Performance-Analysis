# Student Performance Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes a student performance dataset to uncover the behavioral, demographic, and social factors that influence academic success and attendance. Rather than just looking at grades, this dashboard visualizes *why* certain trends exist, exploring elements like family background, travel time, and social life. 


## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
* **Power BI:** Data visualization, interactive filtering, and dashboard design.
* **Power Query:** Data extraction, cleaning, and transformation.
* **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions):** Creating custom measures and aggregations.

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Transformation
All data preparation was handled directly within Power BI using **Power Query** to ensure automated and repeatable transformation steps. Key steps included:
* Importing the raw student dataset.
* Handling missing values and ensuring data accuracy across demographic columns.
* Formatting and adjusting data types for precise modeling.
* Structuring the tables to optimize dashboard filtering and performance.

## 📊 Key Insights from the Data
The interactive dashboard allows users to slice the data by gender, address (Urban/Rural), travel time, and enrollment reasons. Key findings include:

* **Family Dynamics & Attendance:** Analyzed the impact of home life on student reliability, revealing that students whose parents live "Apart" have noticeably higher average absences compared to those whose parents live "Together."
* **The Impact of Support Systems:** Identified a strong correlation showing that students who receive both family educational support and extra paid classes have a lower average rate of academic failure.
* **Motivation vs. Absenteeism:** Discovered that the reason a student chooses a school impacts their attendance; students who enrolled primarily because it was close to "home" exhibited higher absentee rates, particularly those coming from rural addresses.
* **Social & Health Factors:** Mapped the relationship between a student's self-reported health status, how often they go out with friends, and their dedicated study time to identify lifestyle impacts on education.
